# Jessie Marie Jacobs

Jessie Marie Jacobs was a famous African American civil rights activist who fought for the rights of African Americans in the United States. She was born in 1912 in Atlanta, Georgia and was an important leader in the civil rights movement. She was a strong advocate for education and equality for African Americans. She was a key figure in the Montgomery Bus Boycott and was a co-founder of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference. She was also a key figure in the March on Washington in 1963. She was a powerful speaker and an inspirational leader. She worked hard to ensure that African Americans had the same rights and opportunities as everyone else. She was a strong believer in non-violent protest and was a tireless campaigner for justice and equality. She was an important role model for future generations of civil rights activists.
